Advanced spam tactics

Modern bots no longer say "BUY" — they masquerade as real people

🕵️‍♂️

"Trojan Horse"

The bot sends a short, friendly message: "Hi! How are you?". No ads in the text, but the profile Bio and Stories link to a scam channel. Simple filters let these through — our AI checks the full profile.

🤖

AI summarization

Spammers use neural networks to generate "helpful" summaries of your posts. It looks like a polite addition to the topic, but the goal is to boost account reputation or lure reactors into DMs. We detect typical text-generation patterns and emoji overload.

🏗️

Native imitation

A group of bots stages an entire show in the comments. One asks for advice on the chat topic (e.g., real estate), another "accidentally" recommends a service or lawyer. It looks like a real discussion, but our AI spots suspicious links between accounts.

🔗

Proxy bots

Instead of a direct channel link, spammers recommend a "helpful assistant bot". This protects their main channel from instant Telegram bans. Our AI analyzes what resources the mentions and links in messages lead to.
